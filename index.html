Maura White · PhD Student in Education Studies · UC San Diego

Studying how AI reshapes learning, identity, and equity

I research how generative AI is reshaping the social and collaborative dimensions of learning in K–12 and higher education, with particular attention to agency, identity, and equity. My work examines how learners and educators interpret and negotiate AI in instructional contexts across writing, human–AI interaction, and school policy.

Research

AI-Supported Writing & Learning

I study how students engage with AI as a collaborator in writing and learning processes, including how they position AI as a peer, tutor, or thinking partner in classroom contexts.

AI Policy & Educational Systems

I examine how schools and districts are developing policies to govern AI use, with attention to how these policies shape access, practice, and equity across educational systems.

Social & Identity Dimensions of AI Use

I investigate how students’ identities, social norms, and moral reasoning shape how they interpret, negotiate, and use AI in learning environments.
